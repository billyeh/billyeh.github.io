Why there is no good furniture

Take shoe benches, and the West Elm Hilma (AKA Nora from Schoolhouse, AKA some Vietnamese name on a local website). You either have the bottom-feeding, imported-from-Asia stuff from Wayfair and Amazon ($100+), or pretty top tier stuff (~$1000). We've squeezed the middle out. You either support a high-end shop with its bevy of administration, execs, designers, and marketers–along with the much lower volumes and higher margins required–or settle for mass-produced knock-offs where you pretty much just pay for the materials, shipping, and markup. I haven't worked it out fully yet, but I suspect the math demands these numbers.

Related: HN thinks that restaurants' introduction of and increasing reliance on robots leads to the same bifurcation between processed junk and high-end boutique "hand-made" items. I agree, and it's well underway already.
